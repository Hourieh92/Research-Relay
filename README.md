In Round 1, participants are divided into small groups of 4–5 people. Each person gives a brief “elevator pitch” style presentation about their research. After all presentations are completed, members of each group anonymously vote for the presentation they think was the best.

In Round 2, the winning presentation from each group moves forward, but it is presented by someone else from the group rather than the original presenter; they can use the board to have drawings if they want. This tests not only how clearly the research was communicated, but also how well others understood it.

After these presentations, all participants vote for the best presentation overall. Both the original researcher and the person who gave the final presentation would receive the prize.
